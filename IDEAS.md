# Chart Smart Ideas

This file holds promising product directions that are intentionally outside Decision Lab v0.1. The current product goal remains one scenario, one polished replay, one memorable pattern reveal, and one strong learning loop.

## Full Detective Mode

Learners draw trendlines, levels, and pattern boundaries before the reveal. A future geometry rubric could compare placement, angle, timing, and evidence with expert reference annotations. This would require careful tolerance rules so the system rewards sound interpretation rather than pixel-perfect imitation.

## Fog of War

Reveal the chart one candle at a time and let learners update their read as evidence changes. The lesson would measure whether a learner adapts without hindsight, overreacting, or anchoring to the first idea.

## Hall of Fame Reasoning

Show selected high-quality explanations from other learners or reviewed experts after a scenario. Comparisons should focus on reasoning quality, uncertainty, and risk framing rather than profit or popularity. Privacy, moderation, and consent would be required before implementation.

## Advanced Chart Smart Coach

Provide personalized feedback across many completed scenarios. The coach could identify recurring decision habits, recommend targeted practice, and explain progress using a transparent scoring rubric. It should never provide personalized investment advice or market predictions.

## Investor DNA

Build a long-term learning profile from repeated decisions. Possible dimensions include patience, risk discipline, trend reading, confirmation awareness, confidence calibration, and tendency to force patterns. The profile should remain educational, explainable, and editable by the learner.

## Pattern Cards And Mastery Tracking

Create concise visual references for each pattern, including qualifying evidence, common false positives, confirmation, invalidation, and volume behavior. Mastery should reflect consistent interpretation across varied examples, not simple card collection or time spent.

## Guardrails For Future Work

- Do not turn learning feedback into financial advice.
- Do not reward trading frequency, winning streaks, or casino-like behavior.
- Validate every scoring rubric with experienced reviewers and real learners.
- Prefer explainable feedback over opaque scores.
- Add complexity only when it strengthens the decision-learning loop.
